Books Read by Medium
Interactive Chart

Audiobook users completed an average of 13.27 books per year, demonstrating that audio is a consistent and sustainable reading format. With listeners finishing more than one book per month on average, audiobooks clearly support regular engagement with literature.
Physical book readers completed an average of 18.54 books annually, reflecting strong and sustained engagement. This suggests that print reading remains a highly immersive and committed form of reading behavior.

Education
SVG

  • The largest portions of audiobook listeners come from college graduates and post-graduate education levels, suggesting that audiobook adoption increases with education.

  • Hypothesis: Audiobooks may better support the lifestyles of college educated and working professionals, who often balance work, commuting, and study, making multitasking friendly formats more practical.
  • Unlike audiobooks, physical books appear more evenly distributed across education levels, suggesting that print has fewer technological or financial barriers.

  • Hypothesis: Students in high school are often required to read assigned texts in print, which may be why physical books are most popular among those in high school.

Average Age
Interactive Chart

Physical book readers have a slightly higher average age, suggesting that print may remain more embedded in long established reading habits.
